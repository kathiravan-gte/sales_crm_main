1. Lead Management Process

From first contact to qualified opportunity

1.1 Lead Capture

Web / API

Leads submitted via /api/public/lead are automatically created in the system with source website.

Manual Entry

Sales reps create leads directly via the Add Lead button on the Leads page. All required fields must be filled.

Bulk Import

Upload CSV files with columns: first_name, last_name, email, phone, company, status via POST /leads/import.

1.2 Lead Qualification

Stage Definition Next Action
New Lead just entered the system Assign to a sales rep within 24 hours
Contacted First outreach made Schedule a discovery call within 48 hours
Qualified Budget, authority, need, timeline confirmed Prepare proposal or convert to deal
Proposal Sent Formal proposal delivered Follow up within 3 business days
Negotiation Terms under discussion Convert to deal and manage in pipeline
Converted Lead became an active deal Manage through Deals pipeline

1.3 Disqualification

Mark as Won/Lost if the lead is unresponsive after 3 follow-up attempts, explicitly declined, or is outside your target market. Add a note in the activity log explaining the reason.

2.2 Deal Hygiene Requirements

  • Every deal must have an owner, closing date, and deal value set before moving past Qualification.
  • Update the deal stage at least weekly. Stale deals (no update in 14 days) must be reviewed.
  • Log at least one activity per week per active deal to maintain a complete interaction history.
  • Set a follow-up date after every customer interaction.

3. Activity Tracking

Every customer interaction must be recorded

3.1 Activity Types

📞

Call

Phone calls, video calls

📧

Email

Emails, LinkedIn messages

📅

Meeting

In-person or virtual

✅

Task

Internal follow-ups, to-dos

3.2 Logging Requirements

  • Log every call, email, and meeting within the same business day.
  • Include a brief description of what was discussed or decided.
  • Always link the activity to the relevant lead or deal for traceability.
  • Mark activities as completed after they are done — don't leave pending items stale.

3.3 Auto-Logged Activities

The following are automatically recorded by the system — no manual entry needed:

New lead created
Lead status changed
Deal created from lead conversion
Deal stage changed

4. Best Practices

Team standards for maximum CRM effectiveness

Data Quality

  • ✓

    Always use a valid, reachable email address for every lead — duplicates are automatically detected.

  • ✓

    Record the lead source accurately — this data drives channel ROI analysis in the Reports section.

  • ✓

    Do not create duplicate leads. Use the search bar to check for existing records before adding.

  • ✓

    Fill in company name for B2B leads — it enables account-level reporting.

Sales Discipline

  • ✓

    Review the Reports dashboard weekly to identify drop-off stages and improve conversion.

  • ✓

    Use the AI score on lead detail pages to prioritize which leads to contact first.

  • ✓

    Set a follow-up date on every deal before closing the browser tab — stale deals lose momentum.

  • ✓

    When losing a deal, log the reason as an activity note — this feeds the retrospective review.

Security & Access

  • ✓

    Always logout when using shared devices or leaving your workstation.

  • ✓

    Do not share login credentials. Each user must have their own account.

Reporting Cadence

Daily

Check pending activities and follow-ups

Weekly

Review pipeline and update deal stages

Monthly

Review Reports dashboard and conversion trends
